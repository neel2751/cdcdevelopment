Types of Property Development Risk

Property development is a complex process that involves many risks. These risks can be divided into two main categories: financial risks and non-financial risks. Financial risks are those that can affect the profitability of a project, while non-financial risks are those that can affect the success of a project. Some of the most common types of property development risk include:

1. Market Risk: This is the risk that the market will not support the project, resulting in a loss of investment.
2. Construction Risk: This is the risk that the construction process will not go as planned, resulting in delays and increased costs.
3. Legal Risk: This is the risk that the project will not be able to obtain the necessary permits and approvals, resulting in delays and increased costs.

Market Risk
Fluctuations in demand, competition, interest rates, and economic conditions.
Financial Risk
Potential cost overruns, unexpected market fluctuations, securing financing.
Construction Risk
Delays due to material shortages, labor disputes, unforeseen site conditions.
Regulatory risk
Navigating complex zoning regulations, building codes, and environmental permits.
Legal Risk
Design flaws, tenant disputes, regulatory non-compliance, or contract disagreements.
Environmental Risk
Protected species on the site, potential environmental impact assessments.
Structural Risk
Analyzes how financing is divided and potential impact on returns for different parties involved.
Costoverrun Risk
Underestimating construction costs, material prices, or labor expenses early on project budget.
